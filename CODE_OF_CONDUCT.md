# 🤝 Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **Practon Alumni Association Platform** community pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## 🌟 Our Standards

### ✅ Positive Behaviors We Encourage

- **Respectful communication** — Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive feedback** — Critique ideas graciously, not people
- **Empathy** — Be patient and understanding with all community members
- **Collaboration** — Welcome contributions from all backgrounds and skill levels
- **Acknowledging mistakes** — Accept responsibility and learn from errors
- **Community focus** — Prioritize what is best for the community as a whole
- **Cultural sensitivity** — Respect the bilingual (Bengali/English) and culturally diverse nature of this project

### ❌ Unacceptable Behaviors

- Harassment, intimidation, or trolling of any kind
- Discriminatory jokes or comments based on protected characteristics
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing) without consent
- Sharing or discussing vulnerabilities publicly without responsible disclosure
- Spam, self-promotion, or off-topic content
- Any conduct that would be considered unprofessional or harmful in a workplace setting
- Political arguments unrelated to the project

---

## 📋 Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## 🌍 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:
- Within all project spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, wikis)
- In public spaces when an individual is officially representing this project
- In private communications when directly related to project matters

## 🚨 Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainer:

**📧 info.salahuddinkader@gmail.com**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter of an incident.

### What to Include in Your Report
- Your contact information (optional, for follow-up)
- Names of individuals involved (GitHub usernames or real names)
- Description of the incident with as much context as possible
- Any screenshots or links if available
- Whether this is an ongoing situation

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines:

### 1. Correction
**Impact**: Minor inappropriate language or behavior.  
**Consequence**: A private, written warning with clarity on the violation and expected behavior.

### 2. Warning
**Impact**: A single incident or series of actions.  
**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Impact**: Serious violation of community standards.  
**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violations, sustained harassment, or severe misconduct.  
**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
